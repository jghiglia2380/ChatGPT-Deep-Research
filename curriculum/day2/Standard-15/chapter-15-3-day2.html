Chapter 15.3: Job Application Process

Application Materials Workshop: Applying concepts through interactive activities

Learning Objectives

  • Analyze job descriptions to identify key requirements and qualifications
  • Create customized application materials for specific positions
  • Identify and correct common application errors
  • Navigate online application systems effectively
  • Evaluate application materials through peer review

Introduction

Welcome to the Application Materials Workshop! Today's Learning Lab gives you the opportunity to apply the job application strategies we learned in our previous session through hands-on activities. You'll analyze job descriptions, create tailored application materials, review sample applications, and practice with application tracking systems. These practical experiences will help you develop the skills needed to navigate the job application process successfully.

Important Note

The skills you develop today will be useful throughout your career, as you'll likely apply for multiple positions over time. Pay special attention to the customization aspects of each activity, as tailoring your materials for specific positions is one of the most important factors in successful applications.

Connection to Day 1

In Day 1, we explored job search strategies, learned how to read job descriptions, and discussed best practices for creating application materials. Today, we'll put that knowledge into practice through hands-on activities that give you real experience with the application process. The techniques you learned for customizing resumes, writing cover letters, and optimizing for ATS will be directly applied in today's learning stations.

Common Application Errors to Watch For

  • Generic materials not tailored to the specific position
  • Grammatical errors and typos
  • Missing or incomplete information
  • Inconsistent formatting or poor organization
  • Lack of specific, quantifiable achievements
  • Failure to address application requirements
  • Inappropriate email addresses or contact information
  • Unprofessional social media references
  • Unexplained employment gaps
  • Exaggerated or false information

Cover Letter Tips

  • Keep your cover letter to one page (3-4 paragraphs)
  • Use specific examples rather than general statements
  • Address the letter to a specific person whenever possible
  • Explain why you're interested in this particular company
  • Customize each cover letter for the specific position
  • Proofread carefully for errors and clarity

ATS Optimization Strategies

  • Include relevant keywords from the job description
  • Use standard section headings (e.g., "Experience," "Education")
  • Ensure consistent formatting and simple design
  • Spell out acronyms at least once
  • Save files in .docx or .pdf format as required
  • Follow all application instructions exactly
  • Complete all fields, even optional ones when possible

Additional Follow-Up Scenarios

Scenario 2: Application Confirmation

You submitted an online application but didn't receive an automatic confirmation. Draft a brief email to confirm your application was received.

Scenario 3: Post-Interview Follow-Up

You completed an interview for the position yesterday. Draft a thank-you email to follow up after the interview.

Follow-Up Best Practices

  • Wait 1-2 weeks after the application deadline before following up
  • Be concise and respectful of the recipient's time
  • Restate your interest in the position
  • Reference specific qualifications that make you a strong candidate
  • Offer to provide additional information
  • Check for perfect grammar and spelling
  • Include your contact information
