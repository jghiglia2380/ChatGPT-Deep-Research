Resume Peer Review

Estimated time: 15-20 minutes

Receiving feedback from peers helps identify blind spots in your resume and provides fresh perspectives on how to strengthen your presentation.

Common Resume Mistakes to Avoid

Before beginning the peer review, familiarize yourself with these common resume pitfalls:

Typos and Grammatical Errors

  • Even small errors suggest carelessness
  • Always proofread carefully
  • Use spelling/grammar tools as backup

Inconsistent Formatting

  • Inconsistent fonts or spacing
  • Irregular alignment/indentation
  • Inconsistent styling (bold, italics)

Vague Language

  • Generic descriptions without specifics
  • Overused phrases without evidence
  • Lack of action verbs/achievements

One-Size-Fits-All Approach

  • Same resume for every application
  • Not highlighting relevant experiences
  • Missing job description keywords

Peer Review Process

Form small groups of 3-4 students and take turns reviewing each other's resumes using the following criteria:

Criteria Elements to Evaluate
Content Are all key components present? Is information relevant and impactful?
Clarity Is the resume easy to understand? Are responsibilities and achievements clearly communicated?
Formatting Is the design consistent and professional? Is the resume easy to scan quickly?
Language Are strong action verbs used? Is the language specific and compelling?
Overall Impact What impression does this resume give in the first 30 seconds?

Feedback Guidelines

  • Be specific and constructive in your feedback
  • Focus on both strengths and areas for improvement
  • Offer suggestions, not just criticisms
  • Consider how the resume would appear to potential employers

Resume Revision Workshop

Now it's time to implement the feedback you received and strengthen your resume. Implementing feedback immediately helps improve your resume while the suggestions are fresh in your mind.

Prioritize Improvements

Based on the peer feedback you received, identify 3-5 specific improvements to make to your resume:

Resume Revision Tools

Use these resources to strengthen specific aspects of your resume:

Action Verb Library

Replace weak verbs with powerful action verbs that convey impact.

Leadership:

Directed, Orchestrated, Spearheaded, Championed, Guided

Communication:

Articulated, Persuaded, Negotiated, Presented, Authored

Analysis:

Assessed, Evaluated, Researched, Examined, Investigated

Achievement Quantifier

Transform basic responsibilities into quantified achievements.

Before:

Helped customers with problems

After:

Resolved an average of 45 customer inquiries per day with 95% satisfaction rate

Metrics to consider:

Percentages, quantities, frequencies, dollar amounts, time saved

Implement Improvements

Using the Resume Builder skill builder, implement the changes you've prioritized. Focus on:

  • Strengthening bullet points with better action verbs
  • Adding quantifiable achievements
  • Improving formatting for consistency
  • Tailoring content to your target position or industry
  • Addressing any gaps or weak areas identified
