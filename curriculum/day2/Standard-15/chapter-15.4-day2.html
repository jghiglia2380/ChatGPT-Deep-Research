Chapter 15.4: Interview Skills and Workplace Readiness

Learning Lab: Applying professional skills in workplace contexts

Learning Objectives

  • Demonstrate effective interview skills through mock interview practice
  • Apply appropriate workplace etiquette in various professional scenarios
  • Identify expectations for professional communication and behavior
  • Develop strategies for successful workplace transitions
  • Create a personal plan for ongoing professional development

Introduction

Welcome to the Workplace Readiness Learning Lab! Today, we'll build on the interview techniques and workplace expectations you learned in Day 1 by applying them to realistic scenarios. Through hands-on activities, you'll practice interviewing, professional communication, and workplace etiquette.

Important Note

This Learning Lab is designed to be interactive and collaborative. For the best experience, work with a partner for the mock interview activities, and approach each scenario as if it were a real workplace situation. The skills you practice today are directly transferable to your future career.

Connection to Day 1

In Day 1, you learned about interview preparation, common questions, and workplace professionalism fundamentals. Today's activities will help you:

  • Practice the STAR method for answering behavioral questions
  • Apply professional communication techniques in various workplace contexts
  • Implement workplace etiquette rules in realistic scenarios
  • Create an actionable plan for your first days in a new position

Station 2: Workplace Professionalism Scenarios

Estimated time: 15-20 minutes

This activity helps you recognize and respond appropriately to common workplace situations that require professional judgment.

Instructions:

  1. Form small groups of 3-4 students.
  2. Review the workplace scenario cards below.
  3. For each scenario, discuss the questions provided.
  4. Select one scenario to role-play, demonstrating both professional and unprofessional approaches.
  5. Identify the key principles of workplace professionalism reflected in each scenario.

Professional Workplace Expectations Framework

The following six professional expectations create a framework for evaluating workplace scenarios. As you analyze each scenario, consider which of these expectations are most relevant and how they apply.

Reliability Consistently meeting deadlines and commitments; being dependable and trustworthy in your work.
Accountability Taking responsibility for your work and actions; owning mistakes and learning from them.
Adaptability Responding positively to changing circumstances; being flexible and open to new approaches.
Collaboration Working effectively with diverse colleagues; contributing to team success and supporting others.
Initiative Identifying and acting on opportunities to contribute; being proactive rather than reactive.
Ethical Behavior Maintaining integrity and honesty in all situations; adhering to professional and organizational values.
Analysis Instructions

For each workplace scenario:

  1. Identify the 2-3 most relevant expectations from the framework above
  2. Explain how each expectation applies to the specific situation
  3. Discuss how balancing multiple expectations might create challenges
  4. Propose a professional response that demonstrates these expectations

Scenario 1: Handling Constructive Criticism

Your supervisor reviews your first major project and provides constructive criticism about several aspects that need improvement. You spent many hours on this project and were proud of the result.

Relevant Workplace Expectations:
Accountability Adaptability Initiative
Discussion Questions:
  • How does accountability apply when receiving constructive criticism?
  • What aspects of adaptability are important in this situation?
  • How could you demonstrate initiative in your response?
  • What potential consequences might result from handling the situation poorly?

Completeness Necessary information included

Station A Email Communication

Professional vs. Unprofessional Examples
Professional Example

Subject: Project Timeline Update - Action Required

Dear Team,

I'm writing to inform you that the deadline for the Williams project has been moved forward to April 15. Please review your assigned tasks and adjust your schedules accordingly.

If this change creates any conflicts with your existing commitments, please let me know by Friday so we can discuss potential solutions.

Thank you for your flexibility,
Sarah

Unprofessional Example

Subject: URGENT!!! Timeline CHANGED!!!

Hey everyone,

FYI - they moved up our deadline AGAIN!!! Now everything is due 4/15. Not sure why management keeps doing this to us. 🙄

You'll need to work faster I guess. Let me know if you can't handle it.

S

Station C Conflict Resolution Communication

Using "I" Statements
Effective "I" Statement Format

1. State your feelings: "I feel..."

2. Describe the situation: "when..."

3. Explain the impact: "because..."

4. Make a request: "I would prefer..."

Example

Instead of: "You always interrupt me in meetings!"

Try: "I feel frustrated when I'm interrupted during presentations because I lose my train of thought. I would prefer if we could establish a system for questions at the end."

Your Turn: Convert These Statements

"You never respond to my emails on time."

"Your constant last-minute changes are making this project impossible."

First 90 Days Plan

Create a strategic plan for your first 90 days in a new position. Research shows that the first three months are critical for establishing yourself in a new workplace.

Days 1-30: Learning Phase
Days 31-60: Contributing Phase
Days 61-90: Value Creation Phase

First 90 Days Success Strategies

  • Listen and observe before suggesting major changes
  • Build relationships across departments
  • Clarify expectations with your supervisor
  • Identify a mentor for guidance
  • Document your learning and questions
  • Meet all deadlines and commitments
  • Seek feedback regularly
  • Track accomplishments from day one
