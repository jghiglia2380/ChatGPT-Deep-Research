Understanding Tax Deductions and Credits - Learning Lab

Apply your knowledge of tax benefits through interactive simulations and case studies

Learning Objectives

By the end of this Learning Lab, you will be able to:

  • Apply knowledge of tax deductions and credits to real-world scenarios
  • Determine when to use standard vs. itemized deductions based on specific situations
  • Analyze the impact of different tax credits on overall tax liability
  • Develop strategic tax planning approaches for various financial situations
  • Create a personalized tax strategy plan aligned with financial goals

Introduction

Welcome to the Learning Lab! Today, you'll apply what you learned about tax deductions and credits by working with a tax strategy simulator and developing a personal tax strategy plan. These hands-on activities will help you better understand how to make strategic tax decisions in the future.

Important Note

The tax scenarios and calculations in this Learning Lab are simplified for educational purposes. Real-world tax situations may be more complex and require professional assistance. Tax rates, deductions, and credits also change regularly based on tax law updates.

Connection to Day 1

Today's Learning Lab builds on these key concepts from Day 1:

  • How tax deductions reduce your taxable income
  • How tax credits directly reduce your tax bill
  • The difference between standard and itemized deductions
  • The distinction between refundable and nonrefundable credits

3

Strategic Tax Planning Challenge

Estimated time: 15-20 minutes

Apply your knowledge to a complex tax situation where you'll need to make decisions about timing expenses, claiming deductions, and utilizing credits to minimize tax liability over multiple years.

Multi-Year Tax Planning Scenario

Meet the Rivera Family

Miguel and Elena Rivera, both 35, are married with two children (ages 7 and 10). They're facing several significant financial decisions over the next three years that will impact their taxes.

Year 1
  • Combined income: $110,000
  • Medical expenses: $10,000
  • Mortgage interest: $8,000
  • Planned charitable donation: $5,000
  • College course for Elena: $4,000
Year 2
  • Combined income: $118,000
  • Medical expenses: $2,000
  • Mortgage interest: $7,800
  • Planned charitable donation: $5,000
  • College course for Elena: $4,500
Year 3
  • Combined income: $126,000
  • Medical expenses: $2,500
  • Mortgage interest: $7,600
  • Planned charitable donation: $5,000
  • College course for Elena: $0
Important Tax Information
  • Standard deduction (Married Filing Jointly): $25,900
  • Medical expenses are only deductible if they exceed 7.5% of Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)
  • Lifetime Learning Credit: Up to $2,000 for qualified education expenses (phaseout begins at $118,000 for married filing jointly)
  • The Riveras can choose when to make their charitable donations and could "bunch" them into a single year
