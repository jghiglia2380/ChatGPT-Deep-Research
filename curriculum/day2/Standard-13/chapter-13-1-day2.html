Introduction

Welcome to the Debt Management Learning Lab! Today, you'll put your knowledge into practice by working through a series of activities designed to help you develop practical skills for managing high levels of debt. You'll analyze debt situations, create personalized debt reduction plans, explore consolidation options, and develop credit rebuilding strategies.

These activities will give you foundational knowledge, but individuals facing serious debt challenges should consult with certified financial professionals.

Station 1: Debt Situation Analysis

Estimated time: 10-12 minutes

In this station, you'll analyze a debt situation and identify key financial indicators that help determine appropriate debt management strategies.

The Rodriguez Family Case

Michael and Sofia Rodriguez are facing significant debt challenges:

  • Combined annual income: $72,000 ($6,000/month)
  • Monthly expenses (excluding debt payments): $4,200
  • Credit card debt: $18,500 (average 22% APR)
  • Medical debt: $7,200 (0% interest but in collections)
  • Car loan: $12,000 (6% APR, $320/month)
  • Student loans: $32,000 (5.5% APR, $350/month)
  • Current FICO credit score: 580

To calculate DTI:

  1. Add up all monthly debt payments: $320 (car) + $350 (student loans) + minimal payments on credit cards (~$370) and medical debt (~$200) = $1,240
  2. Divide by monthly income: $1,240 ÷ $6,000 = 0.2067
  3. Multiply by 100: 0.2067 × 100 = 20.67%
$

Monthly income ($6,000) - Monthly expenses excluding debt ($4,200) = $1,800 available for debt repayment

Assessment guidelines:

  • Manageable: DTI ratio < 15%, no collections, sufficient income for payments
  • Moderate: DTI ratio 15-30%, may have some high interest debt, income allows for payments
  • Severe: DTI ratio 30-50%, collection accounts, high interest debt, limited payment capacity
  • Critical: DTI ratio > 50%, multiple collections, unsustainable debt load

Debt Management Approaches at a Glance:

  • Stricter budgeting: Reducing expenses to allocate more money to debt repayment
  • Debt snowball method: Paying smallest debts first for psychological wins (regardless of interest rate)
  • Debt avalanche method: Paying highest interest debts first to minimize total interest paid
  • Debt consolidation: Combining multiple debts into one loan, ideally at a lower interest rate
  • Debt settlement: Negotiating with creditors to accept less than full payment (damages credit, usually for severe situations)
  • Credit counseling: Working with a nonprofit agency for budget guidance and possibly a debt management plan
  • Bankruptcy: Legal process for discharging debts; significant credit impact, last resort for critical situations
