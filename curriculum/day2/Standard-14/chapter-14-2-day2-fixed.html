Chapter 14.2: Checking Out Charitable Groups

Learning Lab: Analyzing and evaluating charitable organizations

<\!-- Learning Objectives Section -->

Learning Objectives

  • Apply professional tools and techniques to evaluate charitable organizations
  • Develop personal criteria for assessing charitable effectiveness based on your values
  • Create a strategic charitable giving plan that aligns with your priorities
  • Experience the decision-making process of a grant committee
  • Identify warning signs of fraudulent or problematic charitable organizations
<\!-- Introduction Section -->

Introduction

Welcome to the Learning Lab for Checking Out Charitable Groups\! Today, you'll apply what you learned about evaluating charitable organizations through hands-on activities. You'll analyze real nonprofits, develop evaluation criteria, create a personal giving plan, and participate in a mock grant committee. These interactive experiences will help you become a more informed and effective charitable contributor.

<\!-- Important Note -->

Important Note

This Learning Lab focuses on applying critical thinking to charitable giving decisions. While we'll analyze real organizations, remember that all evaluations are educational exercises and should not be considered formal endorsements or critiques of any particular organization.

<\!-- Connection to Day 1 -->

Connection to Day 1

In Day 1, you learned about various methods for evaluating charitable organizations, including financial ratios, transparency measures, and impact assessment. You also explored resources like Charity Navigator, GuideStar, and the IRS Form 990. Today's Learning Lab will give you practical experience using these tools and techniques to make informed giving decisions.
