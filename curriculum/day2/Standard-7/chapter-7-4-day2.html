Consumer Credit Legislation - Learning Lab

Applying your knowledge of consumer protection laws to real-world scenarios

Learning Objectives

By the end of this Learning Lab, you will be able to:

  • Apply your knowledge of consumer credit laws to real-world scenarios
  • Identify potential violations of consumer protection laws
  • Research consumer rights and resources provided by major credit protection laws
  • Create a comprehensive guide to credit law protections
  • Develop strategies for protecting your consumer rights
  • Analyze how understanding these laws impacts your borrowing decisions

Introduction

In today's Learning Lab, you'll apply what you learned about consumer credit legislation to analyze real-world scenarios, research consumer rights, and develop a personal guide for protecting your rights as a borrower. These activities will help you recognize potential violations of consumer protection laws and know what actions to take if your rights are violated.

Important Note

The scenarios in this Learning Lab are simplified for educational purposes. Real-world consumer protection issues may be more complex and might require professional assistance. Consumer credit laws are updated regularly, so it's important to verify current regulations when facing actual credit issues.

Consumer Credit Case Studies

Estimated time: 15-20 minutes

In this station, you'll analyze different case studies to identify which consumer credit laws might apply, what rights the consumer has, and what actions they should take.

Case Study 1: The Misleading Credit Card Offer

Javier received a credit card offer in the mail advertising a "0% APR" in large print. He applied for the card and was approved. When he received the card, the terms and conditions stated that the 0% APR was only for balance transfers, and the regular purchase APR was 24.99%. This information was in small print on the back of the original offer. After making several purchases with the card, Javier was surprised to see high interest charges on his first statement.

Your Analysis

Case Study 2: The Denied Loan Application

Sophia applied for a car loan at a local dealership. Despite having a good credit score and stable income, her application was denied without explanation. Sophia suspects it might be due to her gender or ethnicity. The dealership did not provide any specific reason for the denial, simply telling her that she "didn't qualify."

Your Analysis

Case Study 3: The Mysterious Debt

Marcus checked his credit report and discovered a $2,500 debt from a retail store credit card he never applied for. The account is showing as 90 days past due and has significantly lowered his credit score. Marcus has never shopped at this store and believes this could be a case of identity theft or a credit reporting error.

Your Analysis

Case Study 4: The Midnight Debt Collector

Lena has been receiving phone calls from a debt collection agency at 11 PM and sometimes as late as midnight. The collector often uses threatening language, saying they will "take everything she owns" if she doesn't pay immediately. The debt is from a medical bill Lena disputes - she believes her insurance should have covered it. The collector also called Lena's employer and discussed her debt situation.

Your Analysis
