Chapter 7.1: Understanding the Cost of Borrowing - Learning Lab

Apply key borrowing concepts through interactive activities and simulations

Learning Objectives

By the end of this Learning Lab, you will be able to:

  • Calculate the total cost of various loan options
  • Compare different borrowing scenarios to identify the most cost-effective options
  • Develop a personal framework for making responsible borrowing decisions
  • Apply knowledge about loans to create a strategic approach to borrowing

Introduction

Welcome to the Learning Lab! Today, we'll deepen our understanding of borrowing costs by analyzing various loan scenarios and calculating the true cost of different borrowing options. This hands-on experience will help you make more informed borrowing decisions in the future.

Important Note

The activities in today's Learning Lab use hypothetical scenarios and simplified calculations. In real life, loan calculations may be more complex and include additional fees and factors. Always read the fine print and consider consulting with a financial professional before making significant borrowing decisions.

Connection to Day 1

In Day 1, we covered fundamental concepts about borrowing. Today's activities build on that knowledge by having you apply these concepts in practical scenarios:

  • Principal, interest, and APR calculations
  • Comparing secured vs. unsecured credit options
  • Analyzing installment vs. non-installment credit
  • Evaluating when borrowing is appropriate

Borrowing Decision Simulator

Estimated time: 15 minutes

In this activity, you'll face realistic financial scenarios where you need to make borrowing decisions. For each scenario, consider the purpose of the loan, available options, your financial situation, and long-term implications.

Scenario 1: Car Purchase

You need reliable transportation for your new job that's 25 miles away. You've saved $2,000 for a down payment, and your monthly take-home pay is $2,800. Your other monthly expenses total $1,800.

Option A: Used Car
  • • Purchase price: $12,000
  • • Loan amount: $10,000
  • • 48 months @ 7.9% APR
  • • Monthly payment: $243
  • • Total cost: $11,664
Option B: New Car
  • • Purchase price: $25,000
  • • Loan amount: $23,000
  • • 60 months @ 4.9% APR
  • • Monthly payment: $433
  • • Total cost: $25,980
Option C: No Car
  • • Use public transportation
  • • Monthly cost: $180
  • • Additional travel time: 45 min/day
  • • Save your $2,000
  • • No loan required

Scenario 2: Education Funding

You've been accepted to a certification program that would increase your earning potential by approximately $15,000 per year. The program costs $8,000 and you have $3,000 in savings.

Option A: Personal Loan
  • • Loan amount: $5,000
  • • 36 months @ 11.5% APR
  • • Monthly payment: $165
  • • Total cost: $5,940
  • • Start program next month
Option B: 0% Promo Credit Card
  • • Put $5,000 on credit card
  • • 0% APR for 12 months
  • • Then 21.99% APR
  • • Minimum payment: $150/month
  • • Start program next month
Option C: Wait and Save
  • • Save $500/month
  • • Start program in 10 months
  • • No loan required
  • • Delay higher income by 10 months
  • • No interest cost

Introductory Rates vs. Standard Rates

  • • No annual fee
