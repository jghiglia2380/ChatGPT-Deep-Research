Do I Need to File Taxes?

In this activity, you'll determine whether different individuals need to file federal income taxes based on their income, filing status, age and other factors. Follow the steps below for each scenario.

Instructions:

  1. Read through each individual's scenario carefully.
  2. Use the provided information and the tax filing requirement flowchart to determine if they need to file taxes.
  3. Select the correct answer from the dropdown options.
  4. After completing all scenarios, answer the reflection questions.
  5. Submit your activity for automated and manual assessment.

Scenarios:

Scenario 1: Emily is 17 years old, single, and earned $2,000 from a part-time job. Her parents claim her as a dependent.


Scenario 2: David is 30, married filing jointly, and earned $40,000. His wife Jennifer earned $35,000.


Scenario 3: Maria is 22, single, and a full-time student. She had no income this year.


Reflection Questions:

  1. What are the key factors that determine whether someone needs to file taxes?
  2. How might someone's tax filing requirements change as their life circumstances change?
  3. Why is it important to understand your tax filing requirements each year?

Assessment:

Your activity will be assessed on the following criteria:

  • Accuracy of tax filing determinations (automated)
  • Thoroughness and thoughtfulness of reflections (AI/human evaluated)
  • Completion of all scenarios and reflection questions (automated)
