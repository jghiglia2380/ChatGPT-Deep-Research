Chapter 11.2: Insurance as a Risk Management Tool - Learning Lab

Applying insurance concepts to make informed financial protection decisions

Learning Objectives

By the end of this Learning Lab, you will be able to:

  • Compare different insurance policies and identify their strengths and limitations
  • Calculate the financial impact of insurance choices using real-world scenarios
  • Evaluate insurance needs across different life stages
  • Apply cost-benefit analysis to insurance decision-making
  • Develop strategies for selecting appropriate coverage while managing costs

Introduction

Welcome to the Chapter 11.2 Learning Lab! Today, we'll take the insurance concepts you learned in Day 1 and apply them to practical scenarios. You'll analyze different insurance policies, calculate costs and benefits, evaluate insurance needs across life stages, and develop comprehensive insurance strategies.

Important Note

The insurance coverage needs discussed in this Learning Lab are simulations for educational purposes. When making actual insurance decisions, always consult with licensed insurance professionals who can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.

Connection to Day 1

In Day 1, we explored the fundamental concepts of insurance as a risk management tool. Today's Learning Lab builds on those concepts by applying them to practical decision-making:

  • We'll analyze different types of insurance policies covered in Day 1
  • We'll apply the concept of risk transfer through insurance to real-world scenarios
  • We'll evaluate insurance deductibles, premiums, and coverage limits
  • We'll apply the policy evaluation criteria we learned to compare insurance options
  • We'll incorporate all these elements into a comprehensive insurance strategy

Podcast Review: Insurance as a Risk Management Tool

Station 2: Insurance Needs Throughout Life Stages

Estimated time: 10-12 minutes

In this station, you'll analyze how insurance needs change throughout different life stages. Insurance needs evolve as individuals acquire assets, have dependents, and face different risks.

Life Stage Profiles

Review the following profiles and determine their insurance needs:

Profile 1: Recent College Graduate

  • Age: 22
  • Income: $42,000/year
  • Assets: Used car worth $6,000, $5,000 in savings
  • Debt: $25,000 in student loans
  • Living situation: Renting an apartment with a roommate
  • Dependents: None
  • Employer benefits: Basic health insurance with high deductible

Profile 2: Young Family

  • Ages: 32 and 34
  • Income: $92,000 combined
  • Assets: Home worth $280,000 (mortgage of $240,000), two vehicles, $30,000 in retirement accounts
  • Debt: Mortgage, $8,000 in credit cards, $12,000 auto loan
  • Living situation: Own a 3-bedroom house
  • Dependents: Two children, ages 3 and 5
  • Employer benefits: Health insurance for family, basic life insurance (1x salary)

Profile 3: Pre-Retirement Couple

  • Ages: 58 and 57
  • Income: $135,000 combined
  • Assets: Home worth $425,000 (mortgage of $150,000), vacation property worth $180,000, two vehicles, $850,000 in retirement accounts
  • Debt: Mortgage, $15,000 home equity line of credit
  • Living situation: Own a 4-bedroom house
  • Dependents: One child in college, parents may need future care
