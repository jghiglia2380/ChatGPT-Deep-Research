Learning Lab: Financial Services Selection Project

<\!-- Learning Objectives Section -->

Learning Objectives

  • Analyze and compare different types of financial service providers
  • Evaluate factors like fees, interest rates, accessibility, and services when choosing providers
  • Calculate the long-term impact of fees and rates on financial outcomes
  • Create personalized plans for selecting financial service providers aligned with specific financial goals

Station 2: Decision-Making Simulation

<\!-- Activity Timer -->
Estimated time: 10 minutes
<\!-- Station Content -->

In this activity, you'll analyze real-world financial scenarios and make recommendations about which specific financial service providers would best meet the needs presented in each situation.

Instructions:

  1. Review each of the scenarios below
  2. Analyze the financial needs and challenges in each situation
  3. Make specific recommendations (include actual provider names)
  4. Justify your recommendations based on the scenario details

Tip for Making Recommendations:

Think about specific financial institutions you're familiar with and recommend actual provider names based on their services, fees, and features that would best address the needs in each scenario.

<\!-- Scenario 1 -->

Scenario 1: The New Small Business Owner

Marcus recently launched his graphic design business after freelancing for several years. He now has three employees and is looking to formalize his business finances. He needs a business checking account, a business credit card, a payment processing solution for client invoices, and potentially a small business line of credit to manage cash flow between client projects. He values easy integration with his accounting software and low fees while he grows his business.

<\!-- Scenario 2 -->

Scenario 2: The International Student

Sophia is an international student from Brazil who just arrived in the United States to start her four-year college program. She receives funds from her parents quarterly and needs a way to manage her money in the U.S. while minimizing international transfer fees. She needs a checking account, a way to pay her rent and other bills, and ideally a debit card that doesn't charge foreign transaction fees when she travels home during breaks. She has no U.S. credit history but will need to start building one.

<\!-- Scenario 3 -->

Scenario 3: The Newly Retired Couple

Robert and Linda just retired after successful careers in education. They have a combined retirement savings of approximately $1.2 million across various accounts (401(k)s, IRAs, and some individual stocks). They own their home outright and receive Social Security benefits. They want to ensure their money lasts throughout retirement, potentially leave some for their two adult children, and minimize tax implications. They're moderately conservative with investments but need to maintain some growth to counter inflation.
