Introduction

Welcome to the Time is Money Learning Lab! Today, we'll build on what we learned about savings and investment strategies. You'll design a comprehensive financial plan that considers time horizons, risk tolerance, and diversification strategies. This lab will give you hands-on experience creating investment plans that are tailored to specific scenarios and goals.

Research Zone: Understanding Investment Options

Estimated time: 10 minutes

Before creating your investment strategy, you need to understand the options available for different time horizons. Explore the investment vehicles below and consider which might be appropriate for different goals and timelines.

Short-Term Investment Vehicles (1-3 years)

Investment Type Risk Level Typical Return Liquidity Best For
High-yield Savings Very Low 1-2% Very High Emergency fund, short-term goals
Money Market Accounts Very Low 1-2% High Short-term saving, liquidity needs
Certificates of Deposit (1 yr) Low 1-3% Medium Known expenses within 1-3 years
Treasury Bills Very Low 1-3% Medium Safe, short-term investment

Notes:

Short-term investments prioritize liquidity and capital preservation over growth. These are best for goals within the next 1-3 years where you cannot risk losing principal.

Medium-Term Investment Vehicles (3-10 years)

Investment Type Risk Level Typical Return Liquidity Best For
Government Bonds Low 2-4% Medium Conservative medium-term growth
Corporate Bonds Low-Medium 3-6% Medium Higher returns with moderate risk
Balanced Mutual Funds Medium 4-7% High Mix of growth and income
Long-term CDs (5yr) Low 2-4% Low Known expenses in 5+ years

Notes:

Medium-term investments balance growth with moderate risk. These are appropriate for goals 3-10 years away, like saving for a house down payment or funding education.

Long-Term Investment Vehicles (10+ years)

Investment Type Risk Level Typical Return Liquidity Best For
Stocks High 7-10% High Long-term growth, beating inflation
Index Funds Medium-High 7-9% High Broad market exposure, low fees
Growth Mutual Funds High 8-12% High Aggressive growth, retirement
Real Estate Investment Trusts Medium-High 5-8% Medium Income and appreciation, diversification

Notes:

Long-term investments prioritize growth over stability. These are best for goals 10+ years away, like retirement or college funds for young children. The longer time horizon allows you to weather market volatility.

Diversification Strategies

Proper diversification helps manage risk while pursuing appropriate returns. Below are sample asset allocations for different risk profiles:

Conservative Profile

  • • 50-60% Bonds
  • • 20-30% Stocks
  • • 10-20% Cash/Short-term
  • • 0-10% Alternative investments

Best for: Near-term goals (3-5 years) or low risk tolerance

Moderate Profile

  • • 40-50% Stocks
  • • 40-50% Bonds
  • • 5-10% Cash/Short-term
  • • 0-10% Alternative investments

Best for: Mid-term goals (5-10 years) or moderate risk tolerance

Aggressive Profile

  • • 70-80% Stocks
  • • 10-20% Bonds
  • • 0-5% Cash/Short-term
  • • 5-15% Alternative investments

Best for: Long-term goals (10+ years) or high risk tolerance

Key Diversification Principles:

  • Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash, alternatives)
  • Diversify within asset classes (different sectors, industries, company sizes)
  • Consider geographic diversification (domestic and international)
  • Adjust allocation based on time horizon and goals
  • Rebalance periodically to maintain target allocation
