Teacher Guide: 1.2 Paying for Post-Secondary Education

Duration: 55 minutes
Content Guide
Learning Lab

Lesson Overview

Standard: Standard 1: Income and Money Management

Chapter: 1.2: Paying for Post-Secondary Education

Duration: 55 minutes

Focus: Understanding various post-secondary education financing options and practical application of financial aid concepts

Materials Needed

  • Digital presentation on education financing options
  • Mock FAFSA worksheets (digital or printable)
  • Student devices with internet access
  • Financial aid comparison chart template
  • Funding priority pyramid visual
  • Case study scenarios handouts

Pre-Class Setup

  1. Prepare digital presentation on financing options
  2. Print or prepare digital access to FAFSA worksheets
  3. Organize case study materials for group work
  4. Bookmark reliable financial aid websites for student research

Lesson Timeline

Time Activity Description
5 min Introduction Engage with education cost realities and financing importance
15 min Key Concept Review Explore different financing options with visual aid pyramid
10 min The FAFSA Deep Dive Explain form importance and completion process
10 min Real-World Case Studies Apply concepts to realistic student scenarios
10 min FAFSA Worksheet Activity Practice analyzing and completing key sections
5 min Reflection & Wrap-up Connect to personal plans and preview Day 2

Detailed Instruction Notes

Introduction (5 minutes)

  • Begin with a hook statistic on average college costs and student loan debt
  • Ask: "How many of you have thought about how you'll pay for education after high school?"
  • Briefly discuss why this topic is critical for all students, regardless of their post-high school plans
  • Set expectations for the day's learning outcomes

Key Concepts (15 minutes)

  • Present the funding priority pyramid (scholarships/grants → work-study → federal loans → private loans)
  • For each funding source, explain:
    • Basic eligibility requirements
    • Application process
    • Typical award amounts
    • Repayment obligations (if applicable)
  • Use visual comparisons to highlight differences between each option
  • Address common misconceptions, especially about loan obligations and scholarship availability
  • Explain how financial aid packages typically combine multiple funding sources

The FAFSA Deep Dive (10 minutes)

  • Explain why the FAFSA is the gateway to most financial aid
  • Walk through key sections using a simplified version
  • Highlight important terminology (EFC, SAR, verification)
  • Discuss deadlines and the importance of early submission
  • Explain dependent vs. independent student status
  • Address privacy concerns students might have about financial information

Real-World Examples (10 minutes)

  • Divide class into small groups
  • Assign each group a case study scenario with different student backgrounds
  • Have groups identify the most appropriate funding sources for their scenario
  • Guide groups to consider both immediate costs and long-term implications
  • Facilitate brief presentations of funding recommendations

Skill Builder Activity (10 minutes)

  • Distribute the mock FAFSA worksheet
  • Guide students through analyzing and completing key sections
  • Focus on interpreting complex questions and required documentation
  • Demonstrate how to use online resources for assistance
  • Provide individual support to students with questions

Reflection & Wrap-up (5 minutes)

  • Have students identify which financing options might be most relevant to their personal plans
  • Discuss common challenges in the financial aid process and strategies to overcome them
  • Preview the Day 2 Learning Lab activities
  • Assign any pre-work needed for Day 2

Discussion Prompts

For Introduction:

"How might financing decisions for education impact your financial situation 5-10 years from now?"

Expected responses: student loan payments affecting housing options, debt-to-income ratio, career choices based on salary potential

For Key Concepts:

"Why might the funding pyramid prioritize scholarships and grants first?"

Expected responses: free money, no repayment, reduces overall cost, less financial burden after graduation

For FAFSA Discussion:

"What circumstances might change a student's financial aid eligibility from year to year?"

Expected responses: parent income changes, number of family members in college, academic performance

Differentiation Strategies

For advanced students:

Assign more complex case studies with multiple interacting factors; encourage research on specialized scholarships in their field of interest

For struggling students:

Provide a simplified funding options chart; work directly with them on FAFSA terminology; use real examples with clear outcomes

For ELL students:

Provide visual flowcharts of the financial aid process; offer translated glossaries of financial aid terms; pair with supportive peers

Assessment Opportunities

  • Monitor small group discussions during case study work
  • Review completed sections of the FAFSA worksheet
  • Use exit ticket questions on key terminology and concepts
  • Observe student participation in funding option discussions
  • Note misconceptions to address in Day 2

Learning Lab Overview

Standard: Standard 1: Income and Money Management

Chapter: 1.2: Paying for Post-Secondary Education

Duration: 55 minutes

Focus: Developing personalized education financing strategies using interactive tools and planning templates

Learning Format: Multi-Activity

Learning Objectives

  • Students will use interactive tools to calculate education funding needs and analyze options
  • Students will develop personalized scholarship search strategies
  • Students will create a comprehensive education financing plan that aligns with their career goals

Materials Needed

  • Student devices with internet access
  • Funding calculator tool (digital)
  • Loan repayment simulator tool (digital)
  • Scholarship search strategy template
  • Financial aid timeline template
  • Education financing plan template
  • Audio equipment for podcast review

Pre-Class Setup

  1. Ensure all digital tools are working properly on student devices
  2. Prepare student loan calculator with sample scenarios
  3. Set up scholarship search resources bookmark list
  4. Prepare podcast audio for review session

Learning Lab Structure

Selected Format: Multi-Activity

Time Activity Description
10 min Review & Introduction Day 1 review podcast and Lab overview
12 min Activity 1: Education Funding Calculator Interactive tool to analyze financing needs
10 min Activity 2: Loan Repayment Simulator Calculate future loan payment impacts
10 min Activity 3: Scholarship Search Strategy Develop personalized scholarship approach
8 min Activity 4: Financing Plan Creation Compile findings into comprehensive plan
5 min Reflection & Wrap-up Plan evaluation and next steps

Detailed Facilitation Notes

Review & Introduction (10 minutes)

Key Day 1 Concepts to Review:

  • Funding priority pyramid structure
  • Different types of financial aid
  • FAFSA fundamentals
  • Financial aid packaging

Podcast Discussion Points:

  • The importance of early planning for financing education
  • Understanding the difference between sticker price and net price
  • The role of education financing in overall financial planning

Learning Lab Introduction:

  • Frame today's activities as building on Day 1 conceptual foundation
  • Emphasize the connections between calculator results and actual decision-making
  • Explain that the end product will be a usable education financing plan

Activity 1: Education Funding Calculator (12 minutes)

Setup Instructions:

  • Students should access the digital funding calculator
  • Provide sample education costs for different institution types
  • Share estimates for typical financial aid packages

Facilitation Steps:

  1. Demonstrate the calculator with a sample scenario (3 minutes)
  2. Guide students to enter their personalized information (2 minutes)
  3. Allow students to experiment with different scenarios (5 minutes)
  4. Facilitate brief sharing of insights and surprises (2 minutes)

Key Teaching Points:

  • Focus on the gap between total cost and expected financial aid
  • Emphasize the importance of estimating all costs, not just tuition
  • Discuss strategies for reducing the funding gap

Monitoring Guidance:

  • Check that students are using realistic cost estimates
  • Help students interpret their calculator results
  • Look for students who may need guidance on institutional options
  • Encourage consideration of multiple scenarios

Activity 2: Loan Repayment Simulator (10 minutes)

Setup Instructions:

  • Students should access the loan repayment simulation tool
  • Provide information on current loan interest rates
  • Display a chart of average starting salaries by career field

Facilitation Steps:

  1. Explain how student loan payments impact post-graduation budgets (2 minutes)
  2. Demonstrate how to use the simulator with a sample loan amount (2 minutes)
  3. Guide students to calculate payments based on their funding gap (4 minutes)
  4. Facilitate analysis of payment-to-income ratios (2 minutes)

Key Teaching Points:

  • The concept of debt-to-income ratio and its importance
  • How loan terms and interest rates affect total repayment
  • Strategic borrowing aligned with expected future income

Monitoring Guidance:

  • Ensure students understand the long-term implications of different loan amounts
  • Help students connect loan payments to their career path salary expectations
  • Look for unrealistic borrowing projections to address
  • Encourage consideration of various repayment strategies

Activity 3: Scholarship Search Strategy (10 minutes)

Setup Instructions:

  • Provide scholarship search strategy template
  • Share list of scholarship search engines and resources
  • Display examples of scholarship categories

Facilitation Steps:

  1. Review different types of scholarships and their requirements (2 minutes)
  2. Guide students in identifying personal strengths and affiliations (3 minutes)
  3. Support students in developing tailored search approaches (4 minutes)
  4. Demonstrate effective application tracking systems (1 minute)

Key Teaching Points:

  • The importance of volume and persistence in scholarship applications
  • How to match personal attributes to scholarship opportunities
  • Strategies for standing out in scholarship applications
  • Creating a sustainable application schedule

Monitoring Guidance:

  • Ensure students are considering diverse scholarship sources
  • Help students develop realistic scholarship application timelines
  • Encourage specific rather than generic strategy development
  • Look for opportunities to suggest specialized scholarships based on interests

Activity 4: Financing Plan Creation (8 minutes)

Setup Instructions:

  • Provide comprehensive education financing plan template
  • Display model example of a completed plan
  • Arrange for individual work with teacher circulation

Facilitation Steps:

  1. Explain the components of a complete financing plan (2 minutes)
  2. Guide students in integrating findings from previous activities (1 minute)
  3. Support students in creating action steps and timelines (4 minutes)
  4. Check for plan completeness and feasibility (1 minute)

Key Teaching Points:

  • Creating a financing strategy is an iterative process
  • The importance of backup options and contingency planning
  • How to create specific, actionable next steps
  • Aligning financing plans with career and educational goals

Monitoring Guidance:

  • Check for alignment between educational aspirations and financial reality
  • Help students identify specific action steps with deadlines
  • Look for gaps in planning that need to be addressed
  • Encourage thinking about both short-term and long-term steps

Reflection & Wrap-up (5 minutes)

Reflection Questions:

  • "What was the most surprising thing you learned about financing your education?"
  • "How might your financing plan affect your educational and career choices?"
  • "What specific action will you take in the next month regarding education financing?"

Connection to Overall Objectives:

  • Reinforce how strategic financial planning enables educational opportunities
  • Connect education financing to career planning from Chapter 1.1
  • Highlight the importance of early planning and research

Preview:

  • Brief introduction to Chapter 1.3 on Income and Taxes
  • Connection between education financing decisions and future income management

Differentiation Strategies

For Advanced Students

  • Challenge them to research specialized loan forgiveness programs in their field
  • Encourage creation of multiple financing scenarios with pros/cons analysis
  • Suggest developing more sophisticated multi-year funding projections

For Struggling Students

  • Provide simplified calculator templates with fewer variables
  • Offer additional guidance during plan development
  • Focus on one or two high-impact financing strategies rather than comprehensive planning

For ELL Students

  • Provide visual guides for using the calculators
  • Offer simplified templates with clear prompts
  • Allow use of financial planning terminology in home language

Assessment Strategies

Formative Assessment Opportunities

  • Monitor calculator usage for understanding of financing concepts
  • Check loan payment calculations for mathematical accuracy
  • Review scholarship strategies for relevance and specificity
  • Observe financing plan development for completeness and realism

Summative Assessment

Deliverable: Education Financing Plan

Assessment Criteria:

  • Thoroughness of cost and funding analysis
  • Realism of financing strategy
  • Strategic approach to scholarship applications
  • Alignment with educational and career goals
  • Specificity of action steps and timeline

Success Indicators:

  • Excellent work: Comprehensive analysis of multiple scenarios, strategic scholarship approach, detailed action plan with contingencies
  • Proficient work: Realistic funding analysis, solid scholarship strategy, clear action steps
  • Needs improvement: Incomplete analysis, vague scholarship approach, limited action steps

Additional Resources and References

  • Federal Student Aid website (studentaid.gov)
  • College Board's BigFuture college cost calculator
  • Fastweb and Scholarships.com search engines
  • Institutional net price calculators
  • Public university tuition and aid comparison charts
