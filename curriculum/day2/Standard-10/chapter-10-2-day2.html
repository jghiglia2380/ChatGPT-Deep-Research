Introduction

Welcome to the Learning Lab for Understanding the Costs and Responsibilities of Renting! Today, you'll apply what you learned about leases, tenant rights, and the true costs of renting through hands-on activities. Lease Agreement Analysis

Estimated time: 15-20 minutes

In this station, you'll analyze sample lease agreements to identify important terms, potential concerns, and negotiation points. Being able to understand lease terms is essential before signing any rental agreement.

Sample Lease Comparison

Below are excerpts from two different apartment lease agreements. Review each section and identify important terms, potential concerns, and negotiation opportunities.

Rent and Deposit Terms

Monthly Rent: $1,500 due on the 1st day of each month.

Late Fee: $75 if rent is not received by the 3rd of the month. Additional $15 per day thereafter.

Security Deposit: $2,250 (1.5x monthly rent). Refundable within 30 days of move-out, less any damages or unpaid charges.

Pet Deposit: $500 non-refundable pet fee plus $50 monthly pet rent per pet.

Application Fee: $75 non-refundable.

Maintenance and Repairs

Tenant Responsibilities: Tenant shall keep premises in clean and sanitary condition. Tenant is responsible for minor repairs under $150.

Landlord Access: Landlord may enter premises with 24-hour notice for repairs or inspections, or without notice in emergencies.

Reporting: Tenant must report maintenance issues within 48 hours of discovery or may be held liable for increased damages.

Termination and Renewal

Lease Term: 12 months from signing date.

Early Termination: Tenant must pay a fee equal to two months' rent plus forfeit security deposit to terminate lease early.

Automatic Renewal: Lease automatically renews for 12 months unless tenant provides 60-day written notice.

Rent Increase: Landlord may increase rent upon renewal with 30-day notice.

Rent and Deposit Terms

Monthly Rent: $1,900 due on the 5th day of each month.

Late Fee: $50 if rent is not received by the 7th of the month. Additional $10 per day thereafter up to $150 maximum.

Security Deposit: $1,900 (equal to monthly rent). Refundable within 21 days of move-out, less any damages or unpaid charges.

Pet Deposit: $300 refundable pet deposit plus $25 monthly pet rent per pet. Limited to two pets.

Application Fee: $50 non-refundable, per applicant.

Maintenance and Repairs

Tenant Responsibilities: Tenant shall maintain the property in good condition. Landlord responsible for all repairs except those resulting from tenant negligence.

Landlord Access: Landlord must provide 48-hour notice before entering premises, except in emergencies.

Reporting: Tenant should report maintenance issues promptly. Emergency maintenance available 24/7 for significant issues.

Termination and Renewal

Lease Term: 12 months from signing date with month-to-month option after initial term.

Early Termination: Tenant may terminate with 60-day notice and a fee of one month's rent.

Renewal: Month-to-month tenancy automatically begins after initial term unless tenant or landlord provides 30-day notice.

Rent Increase: Rent may increase annually with 60-day written notice, not to exceed 5% per year.

Hint: Include first month's rent, security deposit, pet fees, and application fee

Lease Term Dictionary

Reference these common lease terms as you analyze the agreement:

Security Deposit: Money held by landlord to cover potential damages or unpaid rent
Subletting: Renting part or all of your leased property to another person
Joint and Several Liability: Each roommate is fully responsible for entire rent
Holdover: Continuing to occupy property after lease expires
Prorated Rent: Partial rent calculated for partial rental periods

Tenant Rights and Responsibilities Simulation

Estimated time: 15 minutes

Understanding your rights and responsibilities as a tenant helps you navigate common renting challenges. In this simulation, you'll respond to various rental scenarios that test your knowledge of tenant-landlord relationships.

Tenant-Landlord Scenario Simulator

For each scenario below, decide what actions you would take as a tenant. Consider your legal rights, responsibilities, and best practices for documentation and communication.

Your bathroom sink has been leaking for three days, causing water to pool on the floor. You reported it to your landlord when it started, but they haven't responded or sent anyone to fix it. The water is now seeping into the baseboards.

Best Practice Guidance:
  • Send a written follow-up request via email or certified mail
  • Document the leak with photos and videos showing the damage
  • Keep records of all communication with the landlord
  • Check your state's repair and deduct laws if urgent repairs are needed
  • If the issue poses health/safety risks and landlord is unresponsive, contact local housing authority

You've moved out of your apartment after living there for two years. You thoroughly cleaned the unit before leaving and there was only normal wear and tear (a few small nail holes in walls, slight carpet wear). The landlord is now claiming they're keeping your entire $1,800 security deposit for "extensive cleaning and repairs."

Best Practice Guidance:
  • Request an itemized list of all deductions and associated costs
  • Provide your move-out documentation (photos/videos, move-out inspection checklist)
  • Cite your state's law regarding normal wear and tear vs. damage
  • Send a formal dispute letter requesting the return of your deposit
  • If needed, pursue the matter in small claims court (most states have expedited procedures for security deposit disputes)

You and two roommates are on a joint lease. One roommate has consistently been late with their portion of the rent for the past three months, causing tension. They're now saying they want to move out immediately, even though there are 8 months left on the lease.

Best Practice Guidance:
  • Review your lease for joint and several liability clauses (all roommates responsible for full rent)
  • Discuss the situation with your landlord regarding lease modification options
  • Consider a formal roommate replacement process if allowed by your lease
  • Get any agreements in writing, including a release of liability for the departing roommate
  • Create a written payment plan or agreement with documentation of all payments
  • Protect yourself by ensuring full rent is paid even if roommate leaves (you may be liable for their portion)

You receive a notice from your landlord that your monthly rent will increase by $300 (25% increase) in 45 days. You're currently on a month-to-month lease, having completed your initial one-year term. This increase would make the apartment difficult to afford on your current budget.

Best Practice Guidance:
  • Check local rent control laws or rent increase limits in your area
  • Research comparable rental rates in your area to determine market rates
  • Consider negotiating with your landlord (e.g., smaller increase, longer lease term, property improvements)
  • If negotiation fails, develop a moving plan that accounts for application fees, security deposits, and moving costs
  • Give proper written notice according to your lease if you decide to move
  • Begin saving immediately for moving expenses if relocation seems likely

You've noticed your landlord entering your apartment while you're at work without providing any notice. Nothing appears to be missing, but you feel your privacy has been violated. Your lease states that the landlord must provide 24-hour notice before entering except in emergencies.

Best Practice Guidance:
  • Document evidence of unauthorized entry (security camera, witness statements, etc.)
  • Send a formal written letter referencing the specific lease provision requiring notice
  • Request acknowledgment of the privacy policy and confirmation of future compliance
  • Research your state's landlord-tenant laws regarding entry (most require 24-48 hours notice)
  • Consider installing a doorbell camera or similar security device (if allowed by lease)
  • If violations continue, contact a tenant's rights organization or consider legal counsel
