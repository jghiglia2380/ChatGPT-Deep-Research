Chapter 10.1: Renting vs. Owning - Making Informed Housing Decisions

Learning Lab: Applying concepts through interactive activities

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate and compare the total costs of renting versus buying in different scenarios
  • Evaluate housing options using multiple financial and lifestyle criteria
  • Identify the financial requirements for both renting and buying a home
  • Create a personalized housing plan that aligns with your financial situation and goals
  • Apply critical thinking to complex housing decisions with multiple variables

Introduction

Welcome to the Housing Decisions Learning Lab! Today, you'll apply what you learned about renting versus owning through hands-on activities that simulate real-world housing decisions. You'll explore financial calculators, analyze housing scenarios, and develop a personal housing plan based on your unique circumstances and goals.

Important Note

Housing decisions are among the most significant financial choices you'll make in your life. There's no one-size-fits-all answer to whether renting or buying is better—it depends on your personal financial situation, lifestyle needs, and long-term goals. The activities in this learning lab will help you develop a framework for making informed housing decisions.

Connection to Day 1

In our previous class, we explored the fundamental differences between renting and owning housing. Today, we'll build on those concepts in practical ways:

  • We'll use the financial concepts from Day 1 to calculate actual housing costs
  • We'll apply the pros and cons framework to analyze specific housing scenarios
  • We'll extend your housing priorities exercise into a comprehensive housing plan
  • We'll practice evaluating housing options based on both financial and lifestyle factors
