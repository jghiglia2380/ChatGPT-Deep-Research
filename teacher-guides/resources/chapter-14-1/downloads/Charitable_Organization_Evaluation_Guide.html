Charitable Organization Evaluation Guide

Introduction

This guide will help you thoroughly evaluate charitable organizations to ensure your contributions create maximum impact. By researching key aspects of an organization's operations, finances, and effectiveness, you can make informed giving decisions that align with your values and priorities.

Helpful Resources for Charity Evaluation

  • Charity Navigator - Evaluates charities based on financial health, accountability, and transparency
  • GuideStar - Provides comprehensive nonprofit information including financial reports and program details
  • CharityWatch - Independent charity watchdog that rates organizations based on financial efficiency
  • BBB Wise Giving Alliance - Evaluates charities against 20 standards of accountability
  • IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search - Verify an organization's tax-exempt status

Organization Profile

Tip: Always verify an organization's tax-exempt status using the IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search tool. Only donations to qualified 501(c)(3) organizations are tax-deductible.

Revenue Growth Rate (Year-over-Year)
Interpretation Guidelines:
  • Program Expenses: Generally, higher percentages (70%+) indicate more efficient use of funds for mission-related activities
  • Administrative Expenses: While lower is often better, extremely low percentages may indicate insufficient infrastructure
  • Fundraising Efficiency: Lower costs to raise $1 (under $0.20) suggest efficient fundraising
  • Executive Compensation: Should be reasonable given organization size, location, and sector
Important Note: Financial metrics alone don't tell the full story. A newer organization, smaller charity, or one working in a particularly challenging field may have higher overhead costs while still creating significant impact. Consider these metrics alongside program effectiveness and organizational leadership.

Tip: Transparency indicators demonstrate an organization's commitment to accountability. Organizations that openly share information about their finances, governance, and program outcomes typically exhibit better stewardship of donor funds.

Program Effectiveness

Tip: Look for specific, measurable outcomes rather than vague statements about impact. Effective organizations can clearly articulate what success looks like and provide evidence that their programs are achieving meaningful results.
