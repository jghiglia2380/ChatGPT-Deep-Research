Conventional Loans

Key Features

  • Not backed by the federal government
  • Offered by banks, credit unions, and mortgage lenders
  • May be conforming (meet Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac guidelines) or non-conforming (jumbo loans)
  • Fixed-rate or adjustable-rate options
  • Various term options (15, 20, 30 years are most common)

Eligibility Requirements

Requirement Typical Minimum Optimal
Credit Score 620-640 740+
Down Payment 3-5% 20%+
Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI) 43-45% <36%
Mortgage Insurance Required if down payment <20% None with 20%+ down
Employment History 2+ years in same field 2+ years with same employer
Income Verification Pay stubs, W-2s, tax returns Steady income with upward trend

Advantages

  • No upfront mortgage insurance premium (unlike FHA)
  • PMI can be cancelled once you reach 20% equity
  • More flexibility in property types
  • Generally lower total cost for borrowers with excellent credit and 20%+ down
  • Various term options to fit different financial goals

Disadvantages

  • Higher credit score requirements than government-backed loans
  • Higher down payment needed for best terms
  • Stricter qualification requirements
  • PMI can be expensive with minimal down payment
  • Less flexible with credit issues or unique income situations

Best For

  • Borrowers with good-to-excellent credit (680+)
  • Those who can afford a 10-20% down payment
  • Borrowers seeking lower overall costs over the life of the loan
  • Those with stable, well-documented income
  • Buyers interested in different property types (primary residence, second home, investment)

FHA Loans

Key Features

  • Backed by the Federal Housing Administration
  • Designed to help first-time homebuyers and those with limited funds or credit challenges
  • Available from FHA-approved lenders
  • Fixed-rate and adjustable-rate options
  • Primarily for primary residences only

Eligibility Requirements

Requirement Typical Minimum Optimal

580 (with 3.5% down)		 620+
Down Payment 3.5% (580+ credit score)
10% (500-579 credit score)		 10%+

Advantages

  • Lower credit score requirements than conventional loans
  • Lower down payment options (as low as 3.5%)
  • More flexible with recent credit issues
  • Seller can contribute up to 6% toward closing costs
  • May qualify with higher debt-to-income ratios

Disadvantages

  • Mandatory mortgage insurance for all loans
  • Upfront mortgage insurance premium (1.75% of loan amount)
  • Mortgage insurance typically required for the life of the loan (with <10% down)
  • Property must meet FHA standards, which can be strict
  • Limited to primary residences only

Best For

  • First-time homebuyers with limited savings
  • Borrowers with lower credit scores (580-680)
  • Those with higher debt-to-income ratios
  • Buyers with past credit issues that preclude conventional financing
  • Those who need a low down payment option and don't qualify for VA or USDA

VA Loans

Key Features

  • Backed by the Department of Veterans Affairs
  • Available to eligible service members, veterans, and surviving spouses
  • Offered through VA-approved lenders
  • No down payment required in most cases
  • No private mortgage insurance

Eligibility Requirements

Requirement Typical Minimum Details
Military Service Varies by service period Must meet minimum service requirements and have valid Certificate of Eligibility (COE)
Credit Score 580-620 (lender specific) No official VA minimum; lenders set their own standards
Down Payment 0% 100% financing available
Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI) 41% standard, can be higher with compensating factors More flexible than conventional loans
Funding Fee 1.25-3.3% of loan amount Varies based on service type, down payment, and first-time use
Occupancy Primary residence only Must intend to occupy as primary home

Advantages

  • No down payment required
  • No monthly mortgage insurance
  • Competitive interest rates
  • More flexible credit requirements
  • No prepayment penalties
  • Limited closing costs (seller can pay all)

Disadvantages

  • One-time funding fee (which can be rolled into the loan)
  • Limited to eligible veterans, service members, and spouses
  • Primary residence requirement
  • Property must meet VA standards
  • Some sellers may be hesitant about VA loans due to appraisal requirements

Best For

  • Eligible veterans and service members
  • Those with limited savings for down payment
  • Borrowers looking to avoid mortgage insurance
  • Those seeking competitive interest rates
  • Buyers with good but not excellent credit

USDA Loans

Key Features

  • Backed by the U.S. Department of Agriculture
  • Designed for low-to-moderate income borrowers in eligible rural and suburban areas
  • No down payment required
  • Lower mortgage insurance costs than FHA
  • Fixed-rate terms

Eligibility Requirements

Requirement Typical Minimum Details
Property Location USDA-eligible area Must be in a designated rural or suburban area
Income Limits Varies by location Typically cannot exceed 115% of area median income
Credit Score 640 for streamlined processing Can be lower with manual underwriting
Down Payment 0% 100% financing available
Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI) 41% standard (front-end)
41-46% (back-end)		 Can be higher with compensating factors
Mortgage Insurance 1% upfront guarantee fee
0.35% annual fee		 Lower than FHA mortgage insurance

Advantages

  • No down payment required
  • Lower mortgage insurance costs than FHA
  • Competitive interest rates
  • Can finance closing costs if the property appraises above the purchase price
  • Flexible credit requirements with manual underwriting

Disadvantages

  • Geographic restrictions (only eligible in certain areas)
  • Income limits restrict higher-earning households
  • Upfront guarantee fee and annual fee required
  • Primary residence only
  • Property must meet USDA standards

Best For

  • Low-to-moderate income borrowers
  • Those looking to buy in eligible rural or suburban areas
  • Buyers with limited savings for down payment
  • Those seeking lower mortgage insurance costs than FHA
  • First-time homebuyers (though not limited to them)
