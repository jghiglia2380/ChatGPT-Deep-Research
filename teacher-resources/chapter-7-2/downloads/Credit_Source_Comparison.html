Part 1: Credit Source Comparison Chart

Credit Source Typical Interest Rates Common Fees Qualification Requirements Best Uses Advantages Disadvantages
Commercial Banks 4-36% (varies by product) Application fees, origination fees, late payment fees, annual fees (for credit cards) Good-excellent credit score (typically 680+), stable income, low debt-to-income ratio Mortgages, auto loans, personal loans, credit cards, home equity loans
  • Wide range of products
  • Competitive rates for qualified borrowers
  • Established reputation
  • Often have physical branches
  • Stricter qualification requirements
  • May have higher fees
  • Less flexible for those with credit challenges
Credit Unions 3-18% (generally lower than banks) Minimal fees, lower late payment penalties Membership in the credit union, moderate-to-good credit (often more flexible than banks) Auto loans, personal loans, mortgages, credit cards, small personal loans
  • Lower interest rates than banks
  • Fewer and lower fees
  • More flexible lending criteria
  • Not-for-profit status benefits members
  • Membership requirements
  • May have fewer branch locations
  • May offer fewer specialized products
Consumer Finance Companies 10-36% Origination fees, application fees, prepayment penalties Fair-to-good credit (more flexible than banks), proof of income Personal loans, debt consolidation, financing large purchases
  • More flexible approval criteria
  • Quick application process
  • Options for those with less-than-perfect credit
  • Higher interest rates than banks/credit unions
  • May have more fees
  • Terms may be less favorable
Online Lenders 6-36% (varies widely) Origination fees (0-8%), late fees, sometimes no fees Varies widely; some focus on excellent credit, others on fair credit Personal loans, debt consolidation, specialized financing
  • Convenient application process
  • Fast funding (often 1-3 business days)
  • May consider alternative data beyond credit scores
  • Transparent comparison shopping
  • No in-person customer service
  • Variable reputation (research required)
  • May have origination fees
Peer-to-Peer Lending 6-36% Origination fees (1-8%), late fees Minimum credit score typically 600+, income verification Debt consolidation, personal projects, small business funding
  • May approve borrowers traditional lenders reject
  • Fixed rates and terms
  • No prepayment penalties
  • Simple online process
  • Higher rates for lower credit scores
  • Origination fees reduce loan proceeds
  • Longer funding time than some alternatives
Payday Lenders 400-700% APR equivalent Loan fees ($15-30 per $100 borrowed) Proof of income, active checking account, ID (minimal credit check) Short-term emergency expenses when no other options exist
  • Easy qualification
  • Fast funding (same day)
  • No traditional credit check
  • Extremely high costs
  • Risk of debt cycles
  • Short repayment terms (typically 2 weeks)
  • Predatory practices
Retailer Credit 0% promotional to 30% Late fees, sometimes deferred interest penalties Varies by retailer, often more flexible than traditional cards Purchasing specific products from the retailer
  • Special financing offers (e.g., 0% for 12 months)
  • Store discounts and rewards
  • Often easier to qualify for than bank cards
  • High standard interest rates after promotions end
  • Deferred interest can lead to unexpected costs
  • Limited use (only at specific retailers)
