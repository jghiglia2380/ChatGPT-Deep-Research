Interest Rate Options

Type Description Advantages Disadvantages Best For
Fixed Rate Interest rate remains the same for the entire loan term • Predictable payments
• Protection from rate increases
• Easier budgeting 		• Higher initial rate than ARMs
• Rate doesn't decrease if market rates fall
• Refinancing required to take advantage of lower rates 		• Long-term homeowners
• Those who prioritize payment stability
• Buyers during low-rate environments
Adjustable Rate (ARM) Rate is fixed for initial period, then adjusts periodically based on market index • Lower initial rate than fixed
• Potential for lower payments if rates fall
• Good for short-term homeowners 		• Payment uncertainty after fixed period
• Potential for significantly higher payments
• More complex to understand 		• Short-term homeowners
• Those planning to sell/refinance before adjustment period
• Buyers during high-rate environments
Common ARM Types:
• 5/1 ARM: Fixed for 5 years, then adjusts annually
• 7/1 ARM: Fixed for 7 years, then adjusts annually
• 10/1 ARM: Fixed for 10 years, then adjusts annually
Most ARMs have caps on how much rates can increase per adjustment and over the loan life.

Buyer Profile Matching

This table helps match different buyer profiles with the most appropriate mortgage options.

Buyer Profile Recommended Mortgage Options Reasoning
First-time buyer with limited savings
• 640 credit score
• 5% down payment
• Stable income
• Medium-term homeownership plans (5-7 years) 		1. FHA 30-year fixed
2. Conventional 30-year fixed
3. 5/1 ARM (if rates are high) 		FHA allows lower down payment with average credit. Conventional would require PMI but could be a good option if the borrower qualifies. 30-year term keeps payments manageable.
Military veteran
• 680 credit score
• Little savings for down payment
• VA eligible
• Long-term homeownership plans 		1. VA 30-year fixed
2. VA 15-year fixed (if affordable) 		VA loan offers 0% down payment with no PMI, making it ideal for eligible veterans with limited savings. VA also typically offers competitive rates.
High-income professional
• 760 credit score
• 20% down payment
• Excellent income
• Long-term homeownership plans 		1. Conventional 15-year fixed
2. Conventional 30-year fixed (with extra payments)
3. Jumbo (if high-value property) 		With excellent credit, high income, and substantial down payment, this buyer can benefit from a shorter term to save on interest. Jumbo loan if purchasing a high-value property.
Rural homebuyer with moderate income
• 650 credit score
• 5% down payment
• Moderate income
• First home in rural area 		1. USDA 30-year fixed
2. FHA 30-year fixed
3. Conventional 30-year fixed 		USDA offers 0% down payment for eligible rural properties with no PMI. Income must be within program limits. FHA is a good backup option if USDA doesn't work out.
