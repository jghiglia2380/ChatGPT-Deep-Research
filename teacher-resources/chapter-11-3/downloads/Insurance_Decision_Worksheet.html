Decision Framework for Selecting Insurance

This worksheet provides a systematic approach to making insurance decisions by analyzing your needs, comparing options, and evaluating costs.

How to Use This Worksheet

  1. Complete each section in order
  2. Be specific about your personal situation and needs
  3. Use actual numbers when comparing costs
  4. Consider both financial and non-financial factors
  5. Revisit your decisions periodically as circumstances change

Decision-Making Tips

  • Be realistic about usage: Consider your past history and anticipated future needs when evaluating scenarios.
  • Factor in your financial situation: Your emergency fund and financial stability should influence your deductible choice.
  • Consider tax advantages: For health insurance, remember HSA eligibility can provide tax benefits with high-deductible plans.
  • Look beyond price: The cheapest option may not provide the best value or protection.
  • Check for discounts: Many insurers offer multiple discounts that can significantly reduce premiums.
  • Read the fine print: Pay attention to exclusions, limitations, and special conditions.
